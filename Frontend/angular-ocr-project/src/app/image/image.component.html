Extracting Text from Visual Content with Precision

Transforming Images into Editable Text Using Optical Character Recognition Technology

What is OCR?

Optical Character Recognition (OCR) is a technology that converts printed or handwritten text in images into editable and searchable digital text. It is widely used for digitizing paper documents, extracting text from photos, and making text in images accessible and modifiable.

How Does OCR Work?

  • Image Acquisition: A text-containing image is captured using a scanner or camera.
  • Preprocessing: The image is processed to enhance its quality. This may involve adjusting brightness and contrast, removing noise, or correcting the image’s alignment.
  • Segmentation: The image is divided into regions of text, lines, and characters.
  • Character Recognition: Characters within the text regions are identified using machine learning models or pattern recognition techniques.
  • Post-processing: The recognized text is corrected for potential errors and formatted as needed.

Common image formats

Key Image Formats for OCR and Web Applications!!

JPEG (Joint Photographic Experts Group)

Photos and complex images with many details.

PNG (Portable Network Graphics)

Images with transparency, logos, illustrations, screenshots.

GIF (Graphics Interchange Format)

Simple graphics, small animations, icons.
